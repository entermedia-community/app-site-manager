Current Courses

Historical and Contemporary Mayan Cultures

This course offers a profound look into the history of the Maya and an exploration of contemporary Mayans. After completing the online portion of the course, participants will have the option to travel to a rural Mayan community in Guatemala where they will be invited to join in local foods, cultural activities, and engage in volunteer service projects.


Director: Eduardo Tecun

Conversational Spanish

This course provides participants with an opportunity to learn conversational Spanish in one of two Latin American sites. The online portion of this course features interactive videos with a native speaker. The practicum portion of the course consists of a two-week full immersion stay in Guatemala or Chile.

Director: Gabriela Burkey

Digital Library Software Certification

EnterMedia is an opensource digital library software program that permits users to efficiently organize and liberally share the astounding number of digital files accumulated in contemporary society. In this course, participants will meet with the developers of EnterMedia for regular conversations on how to customize and expand the platform worldwide.

Director: Christopher Burkey

Sustainable Methods of Growth (no SMOG)

In this course, participants will jump into the study of eco-sustainable international urbanscape design. Learn how to put permaculture design into practice and collaborate with other designers from around the world on a shared vision of sustainability, urban tranquility, and the holistic cultivation of plants.

Director: Ali Agus
